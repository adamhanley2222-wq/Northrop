# Northrop Consulting Group
## Annual Strategic Plan FY2026

**Financial Year:** FY2026  
**Prepared for:** Managing Director and Executive Leadership Team  
**Date:** 1 July 2025

---

# 1. Executive Overview

FY2026 is a year of disciplined growth, delivery reliability, and leadership accountability. The business enters the year with strong market demand in selected sectors, but margin pressure, uneven utilisation, and increasing delivery complexity across regions.

The strategic priority for FY2026 is to improve execution quality while maintaining profitable growth. This requires better operational visibility, stronger cross-division coordination, and clearer accountability at division and business unit level.

# 3. Strategic Pillars

## Pillar 1: Profitable Growth

### Objective 1.1: Improve quality of revenue
Increase the proportion of revenue from higher-margin work and reduce acceptance of under-scoped or low-quality engagements.

**Measures**
- Gross margin improvement of 2 percentage points by Q4
- Minimum bid margin discipline adopted across all divisions
- Reduction in write-downs on fixed-fee projects

### Objective 1.2: Grow key client relationships
Deepen wallet share with top 20 clients across property, infrastructure, and public sector segments.

**Measures**
- Top 20 client revenue growth >10%
- At least 8 cross-division pursuits by year end
- Improved executive sponsor coverage on strategic accounts

---

## Pillar 2: Delivery Excellence

### Objective 2.1: Improve delivery predictability
Reduce project slippage, late design changes, and rework through better planning and early issue escalation.

**Measures**
- 20% reduction in project schedule slippage
- Fewer than 5 critical delivery incidents per quarter
- Quarterly reporting on major-project risk trend

### Objective 2.2: Strengthen project controls
Lift consistency in project reviews, forecasting, and action tracking across all regions.

**Measures**
- Monthly project health reviews on all major projects
- Standard review templates adopted in all divisions by Q2
- Forecast accuracy improvement by Q4

---

## Pillar 3: People and Leadership

### Objective 3.1: Build leadership accountability
Strengthen discipline in owning delivery outcomes, utilisation, team health, and corrective action.

**Measures**
- Quarterly accountability reviews by division
- Clear owner assigned to all red/amber issues
- Leadership action close-out rate above 85%

### Objective 3.2: Improve capability and retention
Retain key technical staff and improve succession depth in critical teams.

**Measures**
- Voluntary regretted attrition below 8%
- Capability plans completed for all critical teams by Q2
- Graduate and mid-level development pathways refreshed

---

## Pillar 4: Operational Visibility and Data

### Objective 4.1: Improve management reporting quality
Create more consistent and decision-useful quarterly reporting across divisions.

**Measures**
- Standard report template used by all divisions from Q1
- Better linkage between strategy, performance, and actions
- Reduced ambiguity in status reporting

### Objective 4.2: Improve utilisation and forecasting visibility
Provide earlier signals on capacity pressure, underutilisation, and margin risk.

**Measures**
- Weekly utilisation dashboards in all divisions by Q2
- Forecast risk commentary included in quarterly reports
- Action plans for teams below utilisation target

# 4. Strategic Risks

## Risk 1: Margin erosion through poor project discipline
There is a risk that growth continues without sufficient bid discipline, scope control, and commercial review, resulting in revenue growth without corresponding margin improvement.

**Owner:** CFO and all Division Leads  
**Severity:** High

## Risk 2: Delivery slippage on major projects
There is a risk that complex projects continue to experience schedule drift due to poor coordination, slow escalation, and insufficient project controls.

**Owner:** Executive Lead, Project Management  
**Severity:** High

## Risk 3: Uneven leadership accountability
There is a risk that issues are reported but not owned decisively, resulting in repeated amber/red conditions without timely corrective action.

**Owner:** Managing Director  
**Severity:** Medium-High

## Risk 4: Inconsistent reporting quality
There is a risk that quarterly reports remain descriptive rather than decision-useful, reducing the executive team’s ability to identify real issues early.

**Owner:** COO  
**Severity:** Medium

# 5. FY2026 Priority Initiatives

## Initiative A: Major Project Review Cadence
Implement monthly major-project reviews for all projects over threshold value.

**Sponsor:** Michael O’Connor  
**Linked Objective:** 2.1 Improve delivery predictability

## Initiative B: Bid Margin Governance
Introduce mandatory commercial review for low-margin pursuits.

**Sponsor:** David Chen  
**Linked Objective:** 1.1 Improve quality of revenue

## Initiative C: Leadership Accountability Review
Run quarterly executive reviews of open amber/red issues and overdue actions.

**Sponsor:** Managing Director  
**Linked Objective:** 3.1 Build leadership accountability

## Initiative D: Reporting Standardisation
Adopt a standard quarterly reporting template linking performance, risk, and actions to strategy.

**Sponsor:** COO  
**Linked Objective:** 4.1 Improve management reporting quality

---

# 6. Executive Expectations for Quarterly Reporting

Each quarterly report must:
- clearly state performance by division
- identify major risks and opportunities
- show actions, owners, and due dates
- reference strategic pillars and objectives where relevant
- distinguish fact from assumption
- surface unresolved issues, not just completed work

---

# 7. Closing Statement

The executive team expects FY2026 reporting to provide decision support, not merely status narration. Division leaders are expected to demonstrate ownership, clarity, and evidence of corrective action where performance is off track.
