# Northrop Consulting Group
## Quarterly Report — Q1 FY2026

**Quarter:** Q1 FY2026  
**Reporting Period:** July to September 2025  
**Prepared for:** Managing Director and Executive Leadership Team  
**Date:** 10 October 2025

---

# 1. Executive Summary

Q1 delivered a solid revenue start to FY2026, with stronger-than-expected new work conversion in Structures and Civil. Overall utilisation was acceptable, but delivery performance was mixed, particularly on a small number of complex projects requiring cross-division coordination.

The quarter showed early progress against the growth agenda, but evidence of improved delivery predictability was limited. Reporting quality improved in some divisions, though action ownership remains inconsistent.

Overall status: **Amber**

---

# 2. Group Performance Overview

## Revenue and Margin
Revenue for Q1 was above budget by 4.5%. Margin performance was mixed, with strong performance in Structures offset by softer returns in Civil and Water & Environment due to project overruns and scope pressure.

## Utilisation
Group utilisation finished slightly below target, with underutilisation in parts of Water & Environment and uneven demand across regional teams.

## Delivery
A number of projects remained on track; however, there were warning signs in programme coordination and late design changes on several larger jobs.

---

# 3. Division Reports

## 3.1 Structures
**Executive Lead:** Sarah Lim  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Stable

Structures recorded a strong quarter in revenue conversion, particularly in commercial and mixed-use work. Team performance was generally solid, and bid activity remained healthy.

However, two major projects experienced early coordination issues, with late client-side clarifications affecting programme certainty. These issues have not yet materially impacted revenue, but they may affect delivery predictability in Q2 if not addressed.

### Key Points
- revenue above budget
- margin slightly above target
- pipeline remains strong
- early signs of programme slippage on two major projects

### Risks
- project coordination on two major jobs remains fragile
- documentation quality under deadline pressure could lead to rework

### Opportunities
- cross-sell with Project Management on two large mixed-use developments
- expand role with an existing property client on repeat work

### Actions
- establish fortnightly review on at-risk major projects
- assign named project controls support to the two flagged projects

---

## 3.2 Civil
**Executive Lead:** David Chen  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Worsening

Civil achieved good top-line activity, particularly in land development. Margin quality was below expectation on several pursuits accepted under competitive pressure.

Forecast commentary indicated that some teams are carrying lower-quality work than intended under the profitable growth objective. Bid margin discipline has not yet been applied consistently.

### Risks
- margin erosion through low-fee work
- incomplete commercial challenge on fast-turnaround bids

### Actions
- implement pre-bid review for sub-threshold margin work
- report monthly on exception approvals

---

## 3.3 Water & Environment
**Executive Lead:** Priya Nair  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Stable

Water & Environment experienced soft utilisation in selected teams and variable pipeline confidence. Some work was delayed awaiting client approvals.

Capability and retention risks were discussed at a high level, but there was limited evidence of concrete mitigation plans this quarter.

### Risks
- underutilisation in two specialist teams
- possible retention pressure among mid-level technical staff

### Actions
- review resource allocation across teams
- prepare capability plan for critical technical roles

---

## 3.4 Project Management
**Executive Lead:** Michael O’Connor  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Improving

Project Management made progress in introducing more regular health reviews on major projects. The cadence is not yet consistent across all divisions, but the function has improved visibility on a number of delivery issues.

### Opportunities
- stronger cross-division role in major-project governance
- earlier escalation of risk issues before quarter end

### Actions
- standardise project review template
- expand review cadence to all major projects above threshold

---

# 4. Strategic Alignment Commentary

## Profitable Growth
There was evidence of revenue growth, but less evidence that revenue quality improved consistently. Civil remains the clearest concern under this pillar.

## Delivery Excellence
The quarter surfaced delivery issues, but the evidence of corrective controls was still emerging. Project Management reported progress, though predictability remains only partially improved.

## People and Leadership
Leadership accountability improved in language, but action ownership in reports is still inconsistent. Capability and retention issues were mentioned in Water & Environment without strong supporting actions.

## Operational Visibility and Data
Reporting structure improved modestly, though several sections remain descriptive and do not clearly link performance to actions or strategic objectives.

---

# 5. Group Risks

## Risk: Margin quality deterioration in Civil
**Severity:** High  
Lower-quality pursuits and incomplete commercial challenge are affecting alignment with the profitable growth agenda.

## Risk: Emerging schedule pressure in Structures major projects
**Severity:** Medium-High  
Programme certainty is weakening on two major jobs.

## Risk: Inconsistent action ownership in quarterly reporting
**Severity:** Medium  
Reports identify issues but do not always assign clear owners and due dates.

---

# 6. Executive Actions for Q2

- tighten bid margin governance in Civil
- embed project health review cadence more consistently
- require explicit owner and due date for all amber/red issues
- improve reporting quality against strategic objectives

---

# 7. Suggested MD Attention Areas

- whether Structures early delivery issues are being controlled tightly enough
- whether Civil growth is diluting margin quality
- whether leadership accountability is improving in practice or only in narrative
- whether reporting quality is improving quickly enough to support executive decision-making
