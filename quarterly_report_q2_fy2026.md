# Northrop Consulting Group
## Quarterly Report — Q2 FY2026

**Quarter:** Q2 FY2026  
**Reporting Period:** October to December 2025  
**Prepared for:** Managing Director and Executive Leadership Team  
**Date:** 15 January 2026

---

# 1. Executive Summary

Q2 showed continued revenue momentum, but delivery reliability and margin discipline remain uneven. Several issues flagged in Q1 persisted into Q2, particularly within Structures and Civil. There was improvement in reporting structure and project review cadence, but corrective actions were not consistently closed out.

Overall status: **Amber-Red**

The executive team should note that recurring issues are now visible across more than one reporting cycle, especially around project predictability, margin discipline, and action accountability.

---

# 2. Group Performance Overview

## Revenue and Margin
Revenue remained above budget, but margin improvement at group level was below target. Civil continued to carry lower-quality work, and Structures experienced additional commercial pressure associated with delivery disruption.

## Utilisation
Utilisation improved in Water & Environment following resource reallocation, but Project Management remained stretched and several specialist resources in Structures were operating above sustainable load.

## Delivery
Major-project review cadence improved, and issue visibility is better than in Q1. However, the underlying delivery challenges on several major jobs have not yet been fully stabilised.

---

# 3. Division Reports

## 3.1 Structures
**Executive Lead:** Sarah Lim  
**Status:** Red  
**Trend:** Worsening

The early coordination concerns raised in Q1 have now translated into measurable programme slippage on two major projects. One project also experienced rework associated with late design clarification and consultant coordination failure.

Revenue remained solid, but delivery predictability clearly deteriorated in the quarter. Margin was negatively affected on one project due to additional unplanned effort.

### Key Points
- strong market demand continued
- revenue remained above budget
- schedule slippage became visible on two major projects
- one project required rework and additional senior oversight
- specialist team capacity is under pressure

### Risks
- unresolved delivery slippage on major projects
- overreliance on a small number of senior technical staff
- risk of further margin erosion if corrective action is delayed

### Opportunities
- stronger integration with Project Management may stabilise governance
- lessons learned review could reduce repeat coordination failures

### Actions
- weekly executive review on the two red projects
- assign dedicated project controls lead to both projects
- conduct post-incident review on rework drivers
- present recovery plan to MD by end of January

---

## 3.2 Civil
**Executive Lead:** David Chen  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Stable

Civil continued to win work but showed only modest progress on bid discipline. Exception reporting has started, but the business is still accepting some sub-target margin opportunities under competitive pressure.

The issue is now better controlled than in Q1, but still not fully aligned with the profitable growth objective.

### Risks
- continued margin dilution from lower-quality work
- teams still incentivised toward volume over quality in some areas

### Actions
- continue exception approval process
- introduce mandatory executive review on materially sub-target bids
- track gross margin quality monthly

---

## 3.3 Water & Environment
**Executive Lead:** Priya Nair  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Improving

Utilisation improved after resource reallocation and pipeline confidence strengthened. However, the capability and retention issue noted in Q1 remains only partially addressed. A capability plan has been drafted but is not yet fully implemented.

### Risks
- retention risk still present in specialist technical roles
- succession depth remains thin in one sub-team

### Actions
- finalise capability plan
- commence targeted retention conversations
- improve visibility of succession risk in Q3 reporting

---

## 3.4 Project Management
**Executive Lead:** Michael O’Connor  
**Status:** Amber  
**Trend:** Improving

Project Management successfully expanded the project review cadence across all major projects above threshold. Visibility and escalation discipline improved materially in Q2.

However, the function is increasingly capacity constrained because more divisions are relying on it to stabilise project controls.

### Opportunities
- stronger governance influence across the group
- improved forecasting discipline from common templates

### Risks
- PM function may become a bottleneck if resourcing is not strengthened

### Actions
- propose additional project controls capacity for Q3
- formalise escalation pathway for red projects

---

# 4. Strategic Alignment Commentary

## Profitable Growth
Revenue remains strong, but quality of revenue continues to lag ambition. Civil improved somewhat but has not yet fully embedded bid discipline.

## Delivery Excellence
This remains the most pressured strategic pillar. Structures has moved from early warning signs in Q1 to visible slippage and rework in Q2. Project Management has improved controls, but the operating issues are not yet resolved.

## People and Leadership
There is clearer action ownership than in Q1, which is positive. However, repeated issues in Structures raise questions about whether leadership intervention occurred early enough. Water & Environment still has only partial evidence of retention mitigation.

## Operational Visibility and Data
Reporting quality improved, with clearer status, actions, and ownership. This is one of the more visible improvements versus Q1. Some KPI evidence remains thin, particularly around utilisation action effectiveness and capability measures.

---

# 5. Recurring and Emerging Risks

## Risk: Delivery slippage in Structures now material
**Severity:** Critical  
This issue was signalled in Q1 and worsened in Q2. It is now affecting predictability and margin.

## Risk: Margin quality in Civil still below strategic intent
**Severity:** High  
The issue is better managed but not yet solved.

## Risk: PM team capacity bottleneck
**Severity:** Medium-High  
The improved governance model may be unsustainable without additional capacity.

## Risk: Partial follow-through on capability and retention actions
**Severity:** Medium  
The issue remains visible but still weakly evidenced in terms of resolved action.

---

# 6. Executive Actions for Q3

- require formal recovery plan for Structures red projects
- review whether Structures leadership support is sufficient at project director level
- tighten commercial discipline in Civil with executive sign-off thresholds
- strengthen PM resourcing to avoid governance bottlenecks
- require clearer evidence of retention action completion in Water & Environment
